About

My name is Suma Shamanna, and I am honored to serve as your Asia-Pacific Regional Vice Chair for Democrats Abroad. I am seeking re-election for a second term to continue this important work. An Oregonian and now based in Bangalore, India, I bring a global perspective and a lifelong commitment to civic engagement and community empowerment.

My journey in public service began in Oregon through grassroots organizing and campaign work for Congressman David Wu. Since moving to India, I have been an active advocate for Americans living abroad. Further, with the additional responsibility of Information Security for Democrats Abroad, as the Chief Information Security Officer, I bring a strong focus on technology, data protection and privacy.

My leadership philosophy is rooted in compassion, inclusivity, and results-driven action. I believe deeply in the power of collaboration, mentorship, and digital tools to strengthen our global community of American voters. Living and working across cultures has equipped me with a nuanced understanding of the Asia-Pacific region’s unique needs and strengths.

I am passionate about continuing to build an inclusive, resilient, and connected network for Democrats Abroad throughout Asia-Pacific—where every voice is heard, and every member is empowered to make a difference.

Vision for the Next Two Years 2025-2027

As we approach the critical 2026 midterms and pivotal local elections, our momentum must continue. If re-elected, I am committed to building on our progress with a clear and actionable vision:

  • Unify and Amplify Regional Strengths: Support Country Committees with tailored resources, strategic mentoring, and innovative digital tools—empowering each team to collaborate more effectively and grow stronger together.
  • Impactful GOTV: Automate routine tasks and strengthen the infrastructure so committees can focus on what matters most—community outreach, local engagement, and voter turnout.
  • Champion Inclusion and Membership Growth: Create flexible, cross-committee and on-demand volunteer opportunities to re-engage current members and reach new ones throughout the region.
  • Strengthen NCC Engagement: Partner with our Non-Country Committee DPCA Voting Representative to expand support and meaningful involvement for Americans in countries without formal committees.
  • Advance Youth Participation: Expand opportunities for young leaders through internships, study abroad initiatives, mentorship programs, and targeted outreach to first-time and younger voters.
  • Promote Transparent, Collaborative Leadership: Foster open communication, welcome your feedback, and work side-by-side with you to accomplish our shared goals.
